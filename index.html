Does the Danish Football Superliga have a Supporter Attendance Problem?

Beginning with the 2016/2017 season of the Premier Danish Football League "SuperLiga", the tournament structure and amount of teams participating have been changed.

Previous seasons have been contested between 12 teams playing each other 3 times in a 33 Round tournament where Team with highest points won the championship and the 2 Teams with lowest points were relegated.
New structure as of 2016/2017 Season includes 14 teams playing each other twice in a 26 round initial tournament, where Top 6 teams continue in a Championship "League" of 10 rounds and Bottom 8 teams continue in a Relegation "League"
This change is targeting 2 main goals, first and foremost to increase popularity of the tournament, which since a peak in the 2013/2014 season has seen a decrease in attendance, and secondly to increase quality level of teams for increased
success in European Tournaments.

Conclusion

It is obvious from the analysis that total attendance has decreased in 2016/2017 vs 2015/16, despite of the new structure. This decrease even more significant when excluding the attendance for "Extra Teams 16/17"
so that comparison is like for like on number of teams. This shows that not only is 2016/2017 attendance significantly lower than 2015/2016, but also lower than any other year in the analysis.
Digging one layer deeper it is noted that the decrease 2016/2017 vs. 2015/2016 is shared by all teams execpt for one "FC Midtjylland"

Comparing across seasons for individual teams it is noted that 4 teams have seen increase from 2010/2011 to 2016/2017, namely "Brondby IF" / "AaB" / "FC Midtjylland" / "Sonderjyske".

Before conclusively labelling the new structure as a failure, it remains to be seen if the "Championship"/"Relegation" part of the new structure will be able to turn the trend when matches are of far more importance.
Furthermore since 4 teams have been able to increase over the period, more analysis on why these teams have seen growth could be beneficial for other teams to possibly copy and turn the trend as well.
